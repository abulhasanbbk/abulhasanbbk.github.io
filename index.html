About Me

I joined the Nuffield Department of Primary Care Health Sciences, University of Oxford, in June 2025 as a Researcher in Machine Learning and Artificial Intelligence. I work under the supervision of Dr. Subhashisa Swain and am a member of the Medical Statistics group. Since Michaelmas Term 2025, I have also been a Research Member of the Common Room at Kellogg College.

I completed my PhD in Natural Language Processing and Biomedical Informatics in 2022 at Birkbeck, University of London, under the supervision of Professor Mark Levene and Dr David Weston. I then undertook postdoctoral research at University College London, where I was supervised by Professor Honghan Wu, with a focus on Deep Learning and Clinical Natural Language Processing.

A Brief Research Statement

My research lies at the intersection of machine learning, natural language processing (NLP), and healthcare informatics. This intersection has provided me with the opportunity to envision technological advancements for the safe deployment of these systems in critical domains through innovation in NLP. I aim to build safer, more reliable AI systems by advancing conditional text generation techniques, developing uncertainty quantification methods, and creating NLP-based verification frameworks. Through my work, I seek to bridge theoretical innovation with practical deployment, ensuring that AI systems are not only powerful but also trustworthy in high-stakes environments.
